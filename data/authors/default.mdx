## Tech Stack

I don't believe in having a fixed tech stack. Frontend, backend, system architecture, high availability design — I've worked across all of them, and I've come to see technology choices as context-dependent decisions, not identity.

More importantly, I think the nature of a "tech stack" is changing. In the AI era, **the ability to rapidly absorb new knowledge and put it to use is itself the most valuable skill**. Knowing a specific framework deeply matters less than being able to pick up the right tool, understand it fast, and ship with it. That adaptability — not any particular technology — is what I consider my real stack.

In practice: I started with Rails, spent time with Django, then settled into the JavaScript/TypeScript ecosystem — Angular, React Native, React, Next.js in production. Node.js on the backend, with infra experience alongside. I've also worked on distributed system design and high-availability architecture when the problem called for it.
